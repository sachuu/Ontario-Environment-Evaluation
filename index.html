Water Quality

Black Oysters are very vulnurable to disturbences in their environment. Which makes them a good indicator of the quality of their surroundings. This is especially notable as there is an observable decrease in their population during the major oil spill that took place in Alberta in 2011. The recovery in their population and increased ratio of pairs per km is a positive indicator. Oil spill cleanup efforts and heightened regulations the water quality has seen consistent improvement.
The recent major oil spills have revealed the difficulty there is in cleaning up pollutants from the water. In comparison to past years, excluding an outlier in 2016, there has been a decrease in pollutants entering our water. Which is consistent with previously observed increases in aquatic wildlife populations, such as the Oyster observed in the previous graph.
